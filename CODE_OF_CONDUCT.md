# Code of Conduct

*or: congratulations, you made me write one of these*

This isn't a legal document. It's a fence. If you can't clear a bar this low the problem isn't the bar. The problem is you showed up to a trans woman's project expecting a debate club and I don't have the patience.

---

## The Short Version

Don't be a bigot. Don't be a creep. Don't make this space unsafe for the people it was built for and by.

If you need further clarification, congrats. This document is about you. If you're already annoyed, good. Sit with that. If you're drafting a rebuttal in your head, double good. I live there rent free now.

---

## The Slightly Longer Version

Chisel is a trans-authored project. Not as a footnote. Not as a "diversity statement" wedged into the README. It's foundational. This tool exists because someone transitioned and wanted a markdown editor that felt like hers. If that bothers you. And I cannot stress this enough. There are approximately ten thousand other text editors. Go find one. I will not be sad about it. I will not even notice.

We don't do:

- **Transphobia, homophobia, biphobia, or any flavor of queerphobia.** This includes "just asking questions," "playing devil's advocate," "I'm just concerned about the children," "but what about women's sports," and the entire genre of "I don't mean to be offensive but." You meant to. We noticed. So did everyone else in the room.

- **Racism, antisemitism, Islamophobia, ableism, fatphobia, casteism, or any other hierarchy of who gets to count as human.** You don't have to understand someone's experience to respect it. If your first instinct is to explain why something isn't *really* racist, you're already part of the problem. I'm already tired of you. We're ten words into your argument and I've already drafted the ban.

- **Harassment, stalking, doxxing, sealioning, and "well actually" behavior that only ever seems to target marginalized contributors.** Funny how that works. Almost like it's not a coincidence. Almost like the pattern is so consistent you could set your watch by it.

- **Tone-policing people who are rightfully angry.** If someone tells you something is harmful, shut up and listen. Your feelings about *how* they said it are a you problem. Take them somewhere else. Journal about it. Call your mom. I don't care. Just don't bring them here.

- **Using "professionalism" as a cudgel.** We're here to build good software, not perform respectability for people who'd rather we didn't exist. If deadnaming is unprofessional to you but the deadnaming *of a deadname* is somehow fine, you've got it backwards. The first one's the problem. Always was. How is this confusing.

- **"Civility" discourse.** Civility is how the comfortable ask the hurting to be quieter. We're not interested. We're also not interested in whatever thinkpiece you're about to link about "free speech" or "open debate." We've read it. It wasn't good then either.

- **The phrase "I'm not transphobic, but."** That sentence has never ended well. It won't start now. Whatever follows the "but" is about to be transphobic. That's how sentences work. You said the quiet part loud and the loud part louder.

- **Unsolicited feedback about the author's transition, presentation, voice, name change, or existence.** This is a markdown editor. You don't need to know my medical history to contribute a PR. Wild concept. Truly revolutionary. Try it sometime.

## What We Do Expect

- **Assume good faith. But don't require it of everyone.** Some people have been burned too many times. They get to be cautious. You don't get to demand their trust just because you personally haven't hurt them yet. That's not how trust works. That's how entitlement works.

- **When you mess up, own it.** *"Oh wow, that was bad. I'm sorry. I'll do better."* That's the whole script. Nobody expects perfection. Everyone expects accountability. Don't make your apology into a performance piece. Don't demand forgiveness like it's a refund. Just fix it and move on.

- **Respect people's names, pronouns, and identities.** Not up for debate. Not up for "concern." Not up for "I'm just trying to understand." If you get it wrong, correct yourself and move forward. Do not make it a thing. Do not make *them* comfort *you* about your mistake. You're an adult. You can handle being wrong for three seconds without needing a support group.

- **Remember that contributors are humans.** Humans have limits, triggers, bad days, family emergencies, and other commitments. If someone steps back, thank them for the time they gave. Don't guilt them for having a life. Don't act like your open source project is more important than their sleep, their health, or their survival.

- **Read the room.** If you're the only one laughing, you're not funny. If you're the only one who thinks a comment was "just a joke," it wasn't. If you're about to say "people are too sensitive these days," you're the sensitive one. You're about to have big feelings about a code of conduct and we all see it coming.

---

## Enforcement

If someone's making the space unsafe, they'll be asked to stop. If they don't stop they're out. If they respond with "can't you take a joke," "this is censorship," or the classic "so much for the tolerant left" they're out immediately. No appeals process. No lengthy debate. Nothing. Gone. I have already done enough emotional labor for one lifetime and I'm not spending any of it explaining basic decency to someone arguing in bad faith.

Reports go to the project maintainer. You won't be punished for reporting in good faith. You *will* be believed unless evidence clearly suggests otherwise. "I didn't *mean* it that way" is not evidence. Intent isn't magic. Impact matters. If you're more concerned about being called a bigot than about whether you actually acted like one, sit with that for a while. Somewhere else.

## Why This Exists

Because too many open source spaces claim to be welcoming while quietly tolerating the people who make them unwelcoming. Because "be nice" and "be excellent to each other" are vague enough to protect everyone except the people who actually need protection. Because the author of this document has been in too many rooms where the rules meant nothing until someone like her broke one.

Because I am, according to my own Bluesky bio, "God's second angriest transsexual." Welcome to what that looks like in practice.

This isn't a nice space. It's a *safe* one. There's a difference. Nice asks you to smile while you're being hurt. Safe asks the person hurting you to leave. We chose safe. If that disappoints you, the disappointment is mutual.

If you want nice, there are plenty of projects that'll let you be comfortable while making everyone else uncomfortable. This isn't one of them. Good luck out there. Or don't. I genuinely don't care which.

Welcome to Chisel. Don't make it weird.
